ISDA SIMM v2.8
When two banks trade an OTC derivative — a swap, a forward, an option — neither clears it through an exchange. If one defaults, the other absorbs losses over the 10-day closeout window. Before 2008, this was wildly under-protected. Regulators responded by mandating Initial Margin — a collateral buffer posted upfront. ISDA built SIMM to compute it: apply stressed risk weights to sensitivities, aggregate using correlation matrices, and get a single dollar number both counterparties agree on. This calculator implements Delta + Vega margin using official ISDA SIMM v2.8 parameters across multiple netting sets.
The problem
Counterparty credit risk
OTC derivatives create bilateral exposure. If one bank defaults, the other bleeds over the 10-day closeout window. Initial margin is the pre-posted buffer.
The model
ISDA SIMM methodology
Sensitivity → × risk weight → × concentration factor → aggregate with correlations → margin. Both sides use identical ISDA parameters so they agree without dispute.
This project
SR 11-7 independent validation
FRE-7801 at NYU. Independently rebuilt SIMM in Python and this dashboard. Stress-tested against historical 99% 10-day moves. Key finding: IR tail coverage dangerously thin at 1.20×.
Delta margin
Linear risk
PV01 for rates, spot delta for FX and equity. Applies to all instruments. The primary component for plain vanilla books.
Vega margin
Volatility risk
Sensitivity to implied vol moves. Applies only to options and vol-sensitive instruments. Can dominate Delta in options-heavy books.
Missing
Curvature + Base Corr
Curvature (gamma/volga for options) and Base Correlation (CDO tranches) are not yet implemented. Recommended next step per SR 11-7.
Central finding: SIMM Delta Margin is conditionally approved. FX (2.50×) and Equity (3.02×) adequately covered. IR (1.20×) breached on worst-case historical move — low average exception rates mask tail vulnerability. SR 11-7 validation surfaces exactly this kind of hidden risk.
